## 9. Likely Examiner Concerns (Forewarned = Forearmed)

1. **Disclaimer of "CHIROPRACTIC"** — already addressed above. Standard.
2. **Translation statement** — already provided.
3. **Likelihood of confusion with existing PURA VIDA marks** — possible but defensible. None of the registered PURA VIDA marks are in Class 044 (medical services). They're in clothing, retail, food. Different consumers, different channels of trade. Standard 2(d) response: "Applicant's mark identifies medical/chiropractic services in Class 044; cited registrations cover unrelated goods in Classes 025/035/etc. Different consumers and different channels of trade preclude likelihood of confusion."
4. **Descriptiveness ("Pura Vida" = pure life)** — possibly raised. Defense: 16 years of continuous use establishes secondary meaning under Section 2(f). Have evidence ready: business records, website archive, customer reviews mentioning the name.

If any of these come up, USPTO gives you 6 months to respond. They're routine and resolvable without an attorney for straightforward cases — but if you get a complex office action, $400-800 with a TM attorney to draft the response is money well spent.
