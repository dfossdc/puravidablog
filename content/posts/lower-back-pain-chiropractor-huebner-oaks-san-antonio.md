## Why Lower Back Pain Develops

The lower back is under constant stress. Here is what actually happens:

**Disc Injury and Degeneration**
The discs between your lumbar vertebrae are shock absorbers. Over time, poor posture, repetitive strain, or acute injury causes them to bulge, herniate, or degenerate. This irritates nerves and creates pain.

**Muscular Imbalance**
Desk work and sedentary habits create muscular imbalance. Your hip flexors tighten. Your glutes and core weaken. This pulls your pelvis out of alignment and creates constant low back strain.

**Spinal Misalignment**
When vertebrae drift out of proper alignment, the discs between them compress unevenly. This accelerates disc degeneration and creates chronic pain.

**Facet Joint Dysfunction**
The small joints between your vertebrae (facet joints) become arthritic and inflamed, creating stiffness and pain — especially when you bend or twist.

**Sacroiliac Joint Instability**
Your sacroiliac joints at the base of your spine can become hypermobile or hypomobile, creating pain that radiates into your hip or leg.

Without proper care, lower back pain becomes chronic. The inflammation turns to scar tissue. The misalignment becomes permanent. The pain that started as a minor ache becomes your new normal.

## Common Lower Back Pain Patterns

- **Disc bulges and herniations** — localized back pain or radiating leg pain
- **Facet joint arthritis** — pain with extension and twisting
- **Sacroiliac joint dysfunction** — pain at the base of spine or hip
- **Muscle strains** — acute pain after heavy lifting or awkward movement
- **Postural pain** — ache that builds throughout the day from sitting or standing
- **Sciatica** — shooting pain down the leg from nerve irritation

## FAQ

**Should I rest or stay active?**
Bed rest actually delays recovery. We recommend staying as active as you can tolerate while avoiding movements that aggravate your pain. Movement accelerates healing.

**What if I have a herniated disc?**
Many herniated discs respond beautifully to conservative chiropractic care without surgery. We aggressively treat the inflammation and take pressure off the nerve. If conservative care is not working after 4-6 weeks, surgery consultation is appropriate.

**Can my lower back pain come back?**
Yes, if you return to the same postural and movement patterns that created it. That is why we teach you exercises and ergonomic habits to prevent recurrence.

**Will I need ongoing care?**
Some patients do benefit from periodic maintenance care — monthly or quarterly visits — to keep their spine aligned and prevent recurrence. Others resolve completely and do not need ongoing visits.

**How is this different from physical therapy?**
Physical therapy focuses on strength and flexibility. Chiropractic care focuses on spinal alignment and nerve function. Both are valuable, but chiropractic addresses the mechanical root cause.
