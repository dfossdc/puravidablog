## Why You Should See a Chiropractor After a Car Accident

Car accidents — even minor ones — place tremendous force on the spine, neck, and soft tissues. In a rear-end collision, your head and neck can snap forward and back in milliseconds, stretching and tearing muscles, ligaments, and joint capsules in ways that don't always show up on standard imaging right away. The adrenaline surge that follows a crash can mask pain for hours or even days, which is why so many people feel "fine" right after an accident, only to wake up two days later unable to turn their neck.

Seeking chiropractic evaluation promptly after a car accident is one of the most important steps you can take for your long-term health and recovery. At Pura Vida Chiropractic, Dr. Dan Foss has extensive experience evaluating and treating auto accident injuries — and helping patients document their condition for insurance purposes.

## Common Car Accident Injuries We Treat

### Whiplash

Whiplash is the most common injury from rear-end collisions. The sudden acceleration-deceleration forces stretch and damage the soft tissues of the cervical spine. Symptoms include neck pain and stiffness, headaches starting at the base of the skull, shoulder pain, arm numbness or tingling, dizziness, and difficulty concentrating.

### Herniated or Bulging Discs

The compressive forces of a crash can cause the discs between your vertebrae to bulge or rupture, putting pressure on nearby nerves. This can cause sharp, radiating pain down the arm or leg, weakness in the extremities, and chronic pain that worsens with sitting or bending.

### Muscle and Ligament Injuries

Sprains and strains of the muscles and ligaments of the neck, mid-back, and low back are extremely common after collisions. These injuries often present as dull, aching soreness that flares with movement and worsens over the first 48 to 72 hours.

### Concussion and Cranial Injury

Chiropractic care — particularly Dr. Foss's specialized [cranial adjusting](/en/services/sot-chiropractic) — can play a meaningful role in recovery from concussion by addressing tension in the cranial bones and cervical spine that contributes to headaches, dizziness, and cognitive symptoms.
