## The Return-to-Sport Timeline

**Acute Phase (Days 1-5)**
Reduce inflammation, protect the injury, begin gentle range-of-motion work.

**Early Healing Phase (Week 2-3)**
Restore mobility, begin strengthening, assess for secondary injuries from compensation.

**Strengthening Phase (Week 4-6)**
Progressive loading, sport-specific exercises, proprioceptive training.

**Return-to-Sport Phase (Week 7+)**
Gradual return to sport-specific activities, testing tolerance, final clearance to return fully.

Timeline varies based on injury severity.

## FAQ

**Should I get an MRI?**
Not always immediately. Most soft tissue injuries heal well with conservative care. If pain persists or function does not improve within 2-3 weeks, imaging is warranted.

**Can I train while injured?**
Sometimes. Depending on the injury, we can work around it while it heals — maintaining fitness in uninjured areas while protecting the injured region. But trying to "push through" serious injury delays recovery.

**How do I prevent this injury again?**
Identify the movement pattern or imbalance that created it. We teach you proper mechanics and design a maintenance program to prevent recurrence.

**Do you work with coaches and trainers?**
Yes. We are happy to communicate with your coach or trainer to coordinate care and ensure proper progression back to sport.
