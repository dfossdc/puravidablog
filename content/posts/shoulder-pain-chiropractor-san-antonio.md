## Common Causes of Shoulder Pain

Shoulder pain doesn't simply appear overnight in most cases. It develops gradually through a combination of factors:

**Structural Misalignment**: When vertebrae in your neck and upper back shift out of their proper position (subluxation), they disrupt nerve function and create muscle imbalances. These imbalances pull the shoulder joint out of alignment, leading to compensatory pain patterns.

**Postural Dysfunction**: In our modern world, desk work and mobile device use have created an epidemic of forward-rounded shoulders. This postural pattern shortens muscles in the front of the chest while lengthening and weakening those in the back, creating a perfect storm for shoulder problems.

**Repetitive Strain**: Athletes, construction workers, and anyone performing repetitive overhead activities are particularly susceptible to shoulder pain. The repetitive motion creates micro-trauma that accumulates over time.

**Nerve Interference**: The nerves that supply your shoulder originate from your cervical spine (neck). Misalignments here can compress nerves, reducing their ability to coordinate muscle function properly.

**Joint Dysfunction**: Sometimes the shoulder joint itself loses its proper arthrokinematics—the smooth gliding movement between joint surfaces. This leads to inflammation, pain, and reduced range of motion.

## Symptoms That Deserve Attention

If you're experiencing any of these symptoms, it's time to seek professional evaluation:

- Persistent aching or sharp pain in the shoulder joint
- Difficulty lifting your arm or raising it overhead
- Pain that radiates down your arm into your hand
- Weakness in the shoulder or arm
- Stiffness, especially upon waking
- Clicking or popping sensations with movement
- Pain that worsens with certain movements or positions
