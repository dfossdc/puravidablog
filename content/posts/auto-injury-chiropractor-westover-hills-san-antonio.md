## The Problem With Waiting After an Accident

Many people are tempted to wait after a car accident. You feel okay now, so maybe the pain will not develop. This is a dangerous assumption.

Here is what actually happens in a collision:

**Acceleration and Deceleration Forces**
Your head and body experience sudden forces that stretch and tear soft tissues before your brain even registers the impact. Whiplash is not about hitting your head — it is about your head and neck moving faster or slower than your body, creating shearing forces.

**Spinal Alignment Disruption**
The impact forces your vertebrae out of position. Bones may not break, but ligaments tear, discs bulge, and nerves get irritated.

**Inflammatory Cascade**
Within hours, inflammation begins building. Within days, inflammation becomes swelling. Within weeks, swelling becomes scar tissue if untreated. Once scar tissue forms, recovery takes much longer.

**Delayed Symptom Onset**
Adrenaline masks pain. Many people feel fine for 24-48 hours, then suddenly develop severe pain, stiffness, and neurological symptoms as the inflammatory response peaks.

This is why early evaluation and treatment are critical.

## Common Auto Injury Patterns

**Rear-End Collisions**
The most common cause of whiplash. Your car is hit from behind, and your head snaps backward then forward, creating massive cervical spine stress.

**Side-Impact Collisions**
Your neck is jerked sideways, creating lateral shearing. This often causes asymmetrical injury patterns.

**Head-On Collisions**
High-velocity, high-severity injuries with greater potential for fracture, disc herniation, and serious soft tissue damage.

**Multiple Trauma**
Some accidents cause both direct impact to your body (e.g., hitting the dashboard) and whiplash, creating complex injury patterns.

## FAQ

**Do I have to go to the emergency room?**
If you have severe pain, loss of consciousness, visible deformity, or neurological symptoms (numbness, weakness), the ER is appropriate. For most fender-benders with mild to moderate symptoms, you can come directly to us.

**How long does recovery take?**
Mild whiplash: 2-4 weeks. Moderate injuries: 4-8 weeks. Severe injuries: 8-12 weeks or longer. We reassess frequently and adjust care based on your healing.

**Can you write medical reports for my attorney?**
Yes. We provide detailed documentation of your injuries, treatment, and prognosis. This supports both insurance claims and personal injury litigation.

**What if I wait a few days before seeing a chiropractor?**
You can, but early treatment is better. Inflammation is minimized when treatment starts quickly. Also, if you eventually pursue a claim, the defense will argue that delayed treatment means your injuries were not serious. Immediate treatment helps your case.

**Will I need imaging?**
If your pain is severe or your injury is clearly significant, imaging helps us identify fractures or serious soft tissue damage. Most whiplash cases respond to care without imaging, but we order it if your symptoms warrant it.

**Can I still drive and work?**
Most accident victims can drive and work within a few days, but we may recommend restrictions based on your injury severity. We guide you on what is safe.
