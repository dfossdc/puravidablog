## The Hidden Cost of Antibiotic Overuse

We prescribe antibiotics at an astonishing rate. Hundreds of millions of prescriptions are dispensed every year in the United States alone, and here is the truth that most patients never hear: **most of those prescriptions are unnecessary.** Viral infections — the common cold, flu, most sore throats — do not respond to antibiotics at all. Yet they are prescribed anyway.

Each time you take an antibiotic, something major happens inside your body that goes largely unmentioned. The antibiotic does not just kill the bacteria causing your infection. It also kills the beneficial bacteria living in your gut — the same bacteria that train your immune system, produce essential vitamins, protect against harmful pathogens, and keep your whole digestive system in balance.

This collateral damage is profound. Your gut microbiome — the community of trillions of microorganisms living in your intestines — is where approximately 70 percent of your immune system lives and trains. Wipe out that community with an antibiotic, and you have just disarmed one of your greatest defenses.

What makes this worse is that antibiotic overuse is creating resistant bacteria. These are pathogens that have evolved the ability to survive antibiotics, making infections harder to treat and more dangerous. The very tool that once saved lives is becoming less effective with each passing year.

## Why Your Immune System Is Your Real Defense

Your immune system is not a backup plan. It is your primary defense against infection — and it is far more sophisticated than antibiotics.

When you encounter a virus or bacteria, your immune system does not just attack blindly. It identifies the threat, mounts a specific response, remembers that pathogen for the future, and even teaches other cells how to recognize it next time. This is why you rarely get the same infection twice. Your body learned.

Antibiotics are a sledgehammer. They kill bacteria — both the bad and the good — without discrimination. Your immune system is a precision weapon. Given the right conditions, it outperforms any antibiotic because it adapts, remembers, and builds lasting protection.

The real question is: what creates the conditions for a strong immune response?

Three things stand out. **Sleep** — during deep sleep, your immune system ramps up production of infection-fighting white blood cells. **Stress management** — chronic stress suppresses immune function through elevated cortisol. **Nervous system balance** — your autonomic nervous system directly controls how aggressively your immune cells respond.

This third point is where chiropractic enters the picture.

## The Nervous System-Immunity Connection

Your autonomic nervous system has two branches: the **sympathetic** (fight-or-flight) and the **parasympathetic** (rest-and-digest). When you are in sympathetic dominance — which is where many of us live thanks to chronic stress, poor posture, and spinal misalignment — your immune system actually gets suppressed. Your body is too busy preparing to run from a tiger to build white blood cells.

The parasympathetic nervous system is where immunity thrives. When you are truly relaxed, your body invests in long-term survival — which includes a robust immune response.

Here is what I see in practice: patients stuck in chronic sympathetic dominance catch colds frequently, recover slowly, and often end up on repeat courses of antibiotics. Patients with balanced, parasympathetic-dominant nervous systems get sick less often and bounce back quickly when they do.

## When Antibiotics Are Absolutely Appropriate

Let me be clear: I am not anti-antibiotic. I am anti-overuse.

There are absolutely situations where antibiotics are necessary and life-saving. A serious bacterial infection — strep throat with complications, a deep wound infection, meningitis, severe pneumonia — these are times when antibiotics are the right choice.

What I am saying is this: most of the time, your body can handle the threat. And each time you avoid an unnecessary antibiotic, you preserve your microbiome, strengthen your immunity, and reduce the risk of resistance.
