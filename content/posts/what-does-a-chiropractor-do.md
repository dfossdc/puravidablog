If you've never been to a chiropractor, you might have a vague idea that they "crack your back" and send you on your way. That picture is incomplete — and for many people, it's keeping them from getting real help.

Let's clear this up.

## The Real Goal of Chiropractic Care

Chiropractic is a healthcare discipline focused on the relationship between the spine and the nervous system. The premise is straightforward: when your spine is properly aligned and moving freely, your nervous system functions without interference. When it's not, pain, dysfunction, and reduced health follow.

Your spine isn't just a stack of bones. It houses and protects your spinal cord — the highway through which your brain communicates with every organ, muscle, and cell in your body. Thirty-one pairs of nerve roots branch off the spinal cord and pass between vertebrae on their way to the rest of your body. When those vertebrae are misaligned, compressed, or immobile, nerve function suffers.

Chiropractors call these misalignments **subluxations**. The goal of a chiropractic adjustment is to correct them — restoring proper alignment, motion, and nervous system function.

## What Actually Happens During an Adjustment?

The "cracking" sound many people associate with chiropractic is called a cavitation. It's caused by gas bubbles releasing from the joint fluid as the joint space opens up — the same thing that happens when you crack your knuckles. It's harmless, and it's not even required for an effective adjustment.

At Pura Vida Chiropractic, Dr. Dan Foss primarily uses the **Sacro-Occipital Technique (SOT)** — one of the most advanced and gentle chiropractic methods available. Instead of high-velocity thrusts, SOT uses specially designed wedge-shaped blocks placed under the pelvis, allowing gravity and the patient's own body weight to gently rebalance the spine over time.

There's no cracking. No twisting. No discomfort. Just a precise, evidence-based correction of the structural imbalances driving your pain.

## What Conditions Does Chiropractic Treat?

The short answer: far more than most people realize. Most patients come in for:

- **Back pain** — the #1 reason people visit chiropractors, and one of the conditions where chiropractic has the strongest evidence base
- **Neck pain** — often driven by poor posture, disc degeneration, or whiplash
- **Headaches and migraines** — a large proportion originate from cervical spine dysfunction
- **Sciatica** — nerve pain radiating down the leg from lumbar compression
- **Herniated discs** — chiropractic can reduce pressure and promote healing without surgery

But chiropractic's reach extends beyond pain. Because the nervous system governs every function in the body, restoring spinal integrity can improve sleep, immune response, digestion, energy levels, and more. Many patients come in for a bad back and leave reporting that their digestion improved, their allergies settled down, or their sleep became deeper.

## What Chiropractic Is Not

Chiropractic is not a quick fix or a temporary pain mask. It's not magic. It's not dangerous when performed by a qualified, experienced provider. And it's not just for people in pain.

The most effective use of chiropractic care is **corrective and preventive** — addressing the structural imbalances before they become debilitating problems. Think of it the way you think about dental care: you don't wait until you have a toothache to visit the dentist. You maintain your teeth proactively because you know that prevention is cheaper, easier, and more effective than treating a crisis.

The same principle applies to your spine.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is the cracking sound necessary for an effective adjustment?**
No. The "cracking" sound (called cavitation) is caused by gas bubbles releasing from joint fluid as the joint space opens up. It's harmless, but it's not required for an effective adjustment. Many gentle techniques, like SOT, produce no cracking at all and are equally effective.

**How long does it take to feel better after a chiropractic adjustment?**
Many patients notice significant improvement after just one or two visits. However, lasting structural correction—where adjustments hold longer and your body maintains proper alignment—typically takes longer. The relief you feel along the way makes the process worthwhile.

**Can chiropractic care replace physical therapy?**
They are complementary approaches. Chiropractic corrects structural misalignments that allow the nervous system to function. Physical therapy strengthens and conditions muscles. Both together produce better outcomes. Many patients benefit from doing both, and Dr. Foss coordinates with physical therapists when appropriate.

**Is chiropractic safe for elderly patients or those with osteoporosis?**
Yes. Advanced techniques like SOT use gentle, gravity-based corrections with no high-velocity thrusts. These methods are particularly safe for elderly patients and those with bone density concerns. Dr. Foss modifies all techniques based on individual health status.

**Will I need chiropractic care forever?**
Not necessarily. Some patients come in with an acute problem, get corrected, and maintain results with occasional maintenance visits. Others benefit from more frequent ongoing care as part of their health maintenance routine. Dr. Foss will recommend a plan based on your specific needs and goals.
