A car accident can change everything in an instant. Even a low-speed collision can cause serious injuries that don't show up on X-rays — and symptoms like neck pain, headaches, and back pain may not appear until days after the crash. If you've been in an accident in San Antonio, seeing an **[auto injury chiropractor](https://puravidasanantonio.com/auto-injury-chiropractor/)** as soon as possible is one of the most important steps you can take.

## Why Chiropractic Care Is Critical After a Car Accident

The forces in even a minor collision are enough to strain muscles, sprain ligaments, compress discs, and misalign the spine. **Dr. Dan Foss at Pura Vida Chiropractic** is experienced in identifying and treating the full spectrum of auto accident injuries. Early chiropractic intervention reduces recovery time and prevents chronic pain from developing.

The delayed onset of symptoms is one of the most important reasons to seek care immediately — even if you feel relatively fine after the accident. The adrenaline response at the time of the accident masks pain, and the inflammatory process that causes symptoms typically peaks 24 to 72 hours later. By the time your symptoms reach their peak, seeking documentation of your injuries for insurance purposes becomes more complicated.

## How Chiropractic Helps After a Car Accident

### Whiplash and Cervical Injuries

Whiplash occurs when the head whips forward and backward violently during a collision, stretching muscles and ligaments beyond their normal range. Dr. Foss evaluates the entire cervical spine for subluxations, soft tissue damage, and nerve compression. His training in **[cranial chiropractic](https://puravidasanantonio.com/cranial-chiropractor/)** allows him to address not just the neck but also how the skull and meninges are affected by trauma — a critical component of post-accident care that most chiropractors overlook.

### Disc Injuries

Impact forces can herniate discs that were already under stress, or create new herniations in previously healthy discs. Dr. Foss uses the **[Sacro-Occipital Technique (SOT)](/en/services/sot-chiropractic)** — a conservative, gentle method that has been shown to reduce disc compression and nerve inflammation without surgery.

### Soft Tissue Injuries

Sprains and strains of soft tissues are the most common auto accident injuries, and also the most frequently underestimated. Untreated tissue can heal with scar tissue and adhesions that cause chronic pain for years. Dr. Foss uses **[SoftWave therapy](/en/services/softwave-therapy)** and **[Class IV laser](/en/services/class-iv-laser)** to stimulate correct tissue healing, reduce inflammation, and minimize scar tissue formation.

### Neurological Symptoms

Numbness, tingling, or weakness in the arms or legs after an accident may indicate nerve compression. These symptoms require prompt evaluation. Dr. Foss performs a thorough neurological examination to determine the origin and severity of any nerve involvement.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How soon after an accident should I see a chiropractor?**
As soon as possible — ideally within the first 24 to 72 hours. Early care reduces inflammation before it sets in and provides immediate documentation of injuries, which is important for any insurance claim. Don't wait for pain to worsen.

**Does my insurance cover chiropractic after a car accident?**
Most auto policies include PIP (Personal Injury Protection) or MedPay coverage that covers chiropractic care regardless of who was at fault. Our office can verify your benefits and work directly with your insurer.

**Can I receive treatment if the accident wasn't my fault?**
Yes. You have the right to receive medical care for your injuries regardless of who caused the accident. Dr. Foss works with injured patients in cases where another driver was responsible and can coordinate with your attorney if you have one.
