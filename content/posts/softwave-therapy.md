## What Is SoftWave Therapy?

SoftWave Therapy uses patented supersonic acoustic waves to stimulate your body's own healing mechanisms at the cellular level. Unlike traditional shockwave devices, SoftWave activates stem cells in the treatment area, triggering genuine tissue regeneration rather than just temporary pain relief.

The SoftWave device is FDA-cleared for the management of pain and inflammation associated with musculoskeletal conditions. It is non-invasive, requires no injections, no anesthesia, and no recovery time.

## How SoftWave Differs from Traditional Shockwave Therapy

Both SoftWave and [shockwave therapy](/en/services/shockwave-therapy) use acoustic energy to stimulate healing, but they work differently. Traditional shockwave uses a focused, high-intensity pulse that is particularly effective at breaking down calcifications and dense scar tissue. SoftWave uses a broader, lower-intensity wave pattern that is better at activating resident stem cells and triggering a regenerative response across a wider treatment area. Many patients benefit from a combination of both modalities.

## What to Expect During SoftWave Treatment

SoftWave sessions are quick, comfortable, and require no downtime. A typical session lasts 10 to 15 minutes. Dr. Foss applies a water-based gel to the treatment area and glides the SoftWave probe over the skin, delivering acoustic pulses to the target tissues. Most patients describe the sensation as a mild tapping or fluttering.

Most patients begin to notice improvement within 1 to 3 sessions. There are no needles, no medications, and no recovery period. A typical treatment protocol involves 4 to 6 sessions, each one week apart, followed by a reassessment.
