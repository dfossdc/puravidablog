## What Is Degenerative Joint Disease?

DJD occurs when the protective cartilage that cushions your joints wears down over time. Once cartilage deteriorates, bones begin to rub together, causing:

- Aching, stiffness, or throbbing pain in the affected joint
- Reduced range of motion and flexibility
- Swelling and tenderness around the joint
- A grinding or clicking sensation with movement
- Worsening pain after activity or prolonged rest

DJD most commonly affects the **spine, hips, knees, and hands**, but can occur in any joint.
