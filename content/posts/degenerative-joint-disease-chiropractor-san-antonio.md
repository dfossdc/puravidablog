## What Is Degenerative Joint Disease?

DJD occurs when the protective cartilage that cushions your joints wears down over time. Once cartilage deteriorates, bones begin to rub together, causing:

- Aching, stiffness, or throbbing pain in the affected joint
- Reduced range of motion and flexibility
- Swelling and tenderness around the joint
- A grinding or clicking sensation with movement
- Worsening pain after activity or prolonged rest

DJD most commonly affects the **spine, hips, knees, and hands**, but can occur in any joint.

## Why DJD Gets Worse Without Treatment

Many patients assume that degenerative joint disease is an inevitable consequence of aging that cannot be meaningfully addressed. This is not accurate. The progression of arthritis is strongly influenced by joint biomechanics — how load is distributed across joint surfaces during movement.

When the spine or extremities are misaligned, pressure concentrates unevenly on certain joint surfaces, dramatically accelerating cartilage breakdown. Correcting these biomechanical imbalances with chiropractic care reduces the rate of degeneration and can significantly relieve pain.

## Common Symptoms of DJD

DJD symptoms vary depending on which joints are affected:

- **Spine** — chronic back or neck pain, morning stiffness, muscle spasm
- **Hip** — groin, outer thigh, or buttock pain; limping
- **Knee** — pain climbing stairs, clicking with movement, difficulty rising from a chair
- **Hands/wrists** — difficulty gripping objects, enlarged knuckles
- **Shoulders** — reduced range of motion, pain with overhead movement
