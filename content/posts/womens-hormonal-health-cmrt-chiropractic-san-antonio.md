## The Reproductive Organ-Spine Map: L2-L5 and Sacrum

Your reproductive organs are controlled by spinal nerves that exit at **L2-L5 (lumbar vertebrae 2-5)** and **S1-S5 (sacral vertebrae)**.

These nerves control:
- Ovarian function (hormone production)
- Uterine contraction (during menstruation)
- Pelvic blood flow (essential for reproductive health)
- Sensitivity of reproductive tissues
- Communication between ovaries and brain

When vertebrae at these levels become subluxated, the nerve signal becomes distorted. Your ovaries cannot communicate properly with your brain. Your uterus loses the ability to contract smoothly. Your body cannot regulate hormones.

Result: period pain, irregular cycles, hormonal mood swings, and often infertility.

This is why so many women with reproductive complaints also have lower back pain — it is the same nerve dysfunction causing both problems.

## The Gut-Hormone Connection

There is another piece: **gut health and hormone metabolism.**

Your liver and gut are responsible for processing and eliminating excess hormones. When your gut is not functioning properly (due to L1-L5 subluxation and poor nerve supply), hormones accumulate in your bloodstream. This causes:
- Estrogen dominance (too much estrogen, too little progesterone)
- Hormonal mood swings and depression
- Severe period cramps (your uterus gets flooded with hormones)
- Acne and skin issues
- Weight gain and metabolic slowdown

CMRT addresses both the reproductive organ nerves AND the digestive nerves, which means it restores both hormonal production and hormonal elimination.
