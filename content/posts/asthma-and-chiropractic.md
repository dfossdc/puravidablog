Asthma is a chronic disease marked by inflammation and narrowing of the airways of the lungs, resulting in the symptoms of wheezing, chest tightness, shortness of breath and coughing. The effects of asthma can range from mild discomfort, to severe difficulty breathing, to death from respiratory failure in extreme cases.

More than 20 million Americans have asthma, including more than 6 million children under 18. Asthma is one of the most common chronic illnesses and one with a serious public health impact. There has been a dramatic increase in asthma cases in both children and adults over the last 30 to 40 years. While the exact cause is unknown, many doctors believe the increase is due to environmental factors.

## The Nervous System Connection to Asthma

What many people don't realize is that the nervous system plays a central role in regulating breathing. The nerves that control bronchial dilation and constriction — the very mechanisms that trigger an asthma attack — originate in the thoracic spine (mid-back). When vertebrae in the thoracic region are misaligned or restricted, they can create interference in the nervous system that affects how the lungs and airways respond to environmental triggers.

This is the foundation of the chiropractic approach to asthma. By correcting spinal misalignments (subluxations) that interfere with the nerve pathways governing respiratory function, chiropractic care may help reduce the frequency and severity of asthma symptoms — especially in children, whose nervous systems are particularly responsive to structural correction.

## How Chiropractic May Help Asthma Patients

Chiropractic care does not "cure" asthma and should not replace prescribed medications like inhalers or controller medications. However, a growing body of research and decades of clinical experience suggest that chiropractic adjustments can provide meaningful support for asthma patients by:

- **Reducing nerve interference** in the thoracic spine that affects bronchial tone and lung function
- **Improving thoracic mobility**, which allows for fuller, more efficient breathing mechanics
- **Addressing rib and diaphragm dysfunction** that can restrict the depth of each breath
- **Supporting the immune system** by reducing overall nervous system stress and improving lymphatic drainage
- **Decreasing systemic inflammation** through the whole-body effects of structural correction

Many patients — particularly children — report that regular chiropractic adjustments lead to fewer asthma episodes, reduced need for rescue inhalers, and better overall quality of life.
