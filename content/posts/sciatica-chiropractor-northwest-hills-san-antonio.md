## What Is Sciatica And Why Does It Happen

Sciatica is not one condition — it is a symptom. Something is irritating or compressing your sciatic nerve, and the nerve is firing pain signals down your leg. Common causes include:

**Piriformis Syndrome**
The piriformis muscle in your buttock can tighten and squeeze the sciatic nerve directly. This is one of the most common causes of sciatica and responds beautifully to chiropractic care.

**Lumbar Disc Herniation**
A disc in your lower back bulges or herniates and presses on the nerve root that becomes the sciatic nerve. This creates radiating pain, numbness, and weakness down the leg.

**Lumbar Spinal Stenosis**
The spinal canal narrows, compressing nerve roots and creating radiating leg pain. This is common in older adults but can happen at any age.

**Sacroiliac Joint Dysfunction**
Instability or misalignment in your sacroiliac joint irritates nearby nerve roots and creates sciatic-like pain.

**Spinal Misalignment**
Vertebrae in your lower back drift out of position, irritating the nerve roots that form the sciatic nerve.

The key insight: sciatica will not resolve on its own. You can manage the pain with medication, but without addressing the underlying cause, the nerve irritation persists and the pain returns.

## FAQ

**Will I need surgery for sciatica?**
Not usually. The vast majority of sciatica cases resolve with proper chiropractic care. Surgery is reserved for cases where conservative care fails or where there is severe stenosis or progressive neurological deficit.

**Should I get an MRI?**
If your pain is severe, rapidly progressive, or associated with significant weakness or bladder/bowel changes, yes. Otherwise, we can start care and order imaging if you are not improving after 2-3 weeks.

**Can I do stretches at home?**
Some stretches help, but others can aggravate your sciatica. We teach you exactly which stretches are safe for your specific condition.

**Is sciatica permanent?**
No. With proper treatment, most sciatica resolves completely. The key is addressing the underlying cause early.

**What if my pain does not improve?**
We reassess at 3-4 weeks. If you are not improving, we order imaging and may refer to a spine surgeon for consultation.
