As a parent, you want only the best for your child. When your baby cries inconsolably, won't latch properly, can't sleep, or can only turn their head in one direction, you search for answers. More and more San Antonio families are discovering that pediatric chiropractic care can offer safe, natural relief for many of these challenges.

But the question almost every parent asks is the same: **Is it really safe to take a baby to the chiropractor?**

The short answer is yes — when performed by a chiropractor with proper pediatric training. Here is what you need to know.

## Why Babies May Need Chiropractic Care

Birth is one of the most physically intense events the human body ever experiences. During delivery — especially in prolonged labors, forceps or vacuum-assisted births, or emergency C-sections — the baby's cervical spine can experience significant tension and misalignment.

These small misalignments, called subluxations, can interfere with the baby's nervous system. The nervous system controls virtually everything in the body: digestion, sleep, immune function, feeding reflexes, and more. When the nervous system functions without interference, the body has its best chance to thrive.

## Common Infant Conditions That May Respond to Chiropractic

### Colic

Colic affects roughly 20% of infants. While often attributed to gas or digestive immaturity, there is growing evidence that spinal tension can be a contributing factor. Several studies have shown that infants with colic who receive gentle chiropractic adjustments cry significantly less after treatment. For exhausted parents, this can be life-changing.

### Breastfeeding Difficulties

If your baby has trouble latching, arches away from the breast, or strongly prefers feeding from one side, this may indicate tension in the neck or jaw. A pediatric chiropractor can evaluate and address these restrictions — often with immediate results at the breast.

### Torticollis

Congenital muscular torticollis occurs when the muscles on one side of the neck are tight from birth, causing the baby to tilt or rotate their head constantly in one direction. Chiropractic care is one of the most effective treatments for torticollis when started early — often working alongside physical therapy for optimal results.

### Flat Head Syndrome (Plagiocephaly)

When a baby always looks or sleeps to the same side because of cervical tension, the skull can develop flat spots on one side. Addressing the underlying cervical restriction allows the baby to move their head freely — reducing positional pressure and giving the skull the opportunity to round out naturally.

### Sleep Problems

Many parents notice that after a series of adjustments, their babies sleep longer and more soundly. This makes sense: when the nervous system is free from mechanical interference, the body can relax more completely. Many infants become noticeably calmer and more comfortable after gentle chiropractic care.

### Recurrent Ear Infections

Some studies suggest that gentle cervical chiropractic adjustments may improve drainage through the Eustachian tubes, reducing the frequency of ear infections in susceptible children. Parents frequently report a meaningful reduction in ear infection episodes after incorporating regular chiropractic care.

This is the most important question for most parents — and it deserves a clear answer.

An infant chiropractic adjustment looks nothing like an adult adjustment. There is no cracking, no twisting, no forceful movement of any kind.

The technique is precise, gentle, and specific. Most babies tolerate it very well — many fall asleep during the treatment.

The adjustments used for infants are completely different in technique and force from those used for adults or even older children. Dr. Foss has been caring for infants and newborns for over 23 years and adapts his approach to each baby's age, size, and specific needs.

Pediatric chiropractic has an excellent safety record when practiced by a properly trained provider. Research consistently shows that complications are extremely rare — far rarer than the side effects of many medications routinely prescribed to infants and children.

Their position is consistent with the clinical experience of practitioners worldwide: when performed by a trained pediatric chiropractor, chiropractic care for infants is safe, effective, and beneficial.

## When Should You Bring Your Baby?

Many parents bring their babies shortly after birth as a preventive wellness evaluation. Others come when they notice specific signs. There is no minimum age — Dr. Foss can evaluate babies from their first days of life.

Signs that your baby may benefit from an evaluation:

- Excessive or inconsolable crying
- Difficulty feeding or latching
- Preference for turning the head in one direction only
- Sleep difficulties
- Frequent ear infections
- Constipation or digestive problems
- Flat spot developing on one side of the head
- Arching or stiffness in the back
