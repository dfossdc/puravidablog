## The Spine-Headache Connection

When the upper cervical spine is misaligned, or when the meningeal system is under tension, the result can be:

- **Cervicogenic headaches** — originating in the neck and radiating to the head
- **Tension headaches** — from chronic tightness in the suboccipital muscles
- **Post-traumatic headaches** — following whiplash or concussion
- **Migraines** — complex neurological events influenced by spinal and cranial alignment

## Common Headache Symptoms

Understanding where your headache comes from is the first step toward lasting relief:

- Pain that starts at the base of the skull and radiates toward the forehead or eyes
- Neck stiffness that accompanies or precedes the headache
- Headache that worsens with specific neck movements
- Sensitivity to light, sound, or smell
- Nausea or visual disturbances with migraines
- Headaches that occur predictably after stress, poor sleep, or prolonged screen time
- One-sided head pain
- Headaches that respond temporarily to pain medication but return
