A pilot study published in the December 2005 scientific journal, Clinical Chiropractic, from the European association, The College of Chiropractors, showed that chiropractic helped subjects in the study with neck pain. The study starts off by noting how common neck pain is by pointing out that more than 70% of people in the developed world will experience neck pain at some point in their lifetimes. In this pilot study, the 21 people who completed the study, were divided into two groups for study. One group was those who had neck pain for less than 7 weeks and the other consisted of those with chronic neck pain of more than 7 weeks duration. Outcomes were measured for values such as pain, disability, and perceptions of improvements in quality of life, as well as levels of anxiety and depression.

The 21 patients who completed the study all received a regime of chiropractic care. The number and frequency of visits were determined by the clinical decision of the individual practitioner rendering care to the study subjects. A standardized outcome measurement was made using a scientific method called the Bournemouth Questionnaire (BQ) for neck pain. The results showed that in the acute group, those with neck pain for less than 7 weeks, all the subjects experienced a decrease in pain with 6 of the 7 reporting a significant improvement. In the group with chronic neck pain of longer than 7 weeks, all but 2 experienced improvement. Most of that group had significant improvement, while one reported no change and one was worse at the end of the study. The acute neck pain patients were usually suffering from more severe pain than were those with chronic pain. Researchers summed up the results by stating, "The results demonstrate a positive effect for chiropractic on symptoms of neck pain. The more chronic the presentation, the more treatments were required to achieve asymptomatic status."

## What Causes Neck Pain?

Neck pain rarely develops in isolation. In most cases it is the result of mechanical dysfunction — vertebrae and joints that are not moving or positioned correctly, creating pressure on nerves and strain on surrounding muscles and ligaments. Common causes include:

- **Cervical subluxation** — misalignment of the vertebrae that irritates joints and nerves
- **Muscle strain and whiplash** — from accidents, falls, or sudden forceful movements
- **Disc herniation in the cervical spine** — pressing on nerve roots causing arm pain and numbness
- **Cervical spondylosis** — age-related degeneration of the cervical discs and joints
- **Text neck and forward head posture** — chronic strain from prolonged device use
- **Tension and stress-related neck pain** — tightness in the suboccipital muscles at the base of the skull
- **Thoracic outlet syndrome** — nerve compression between the neck and shoulder

## Common Symptoms of Neck Pain

Neck pain presents differently depending on its cause and severity:

- Stiffness and reduced range of motion — difficulty turning your head fully
- Dull, sharp, or aching pain in the neck or at the base of the skull
- Headaches that begin at the back of the head and extend toward the forehead or eyes
- Numbness or tingling radiating into the shoulders, arms, or hands
- Arm weakness or difficulty holding objects
- Pain that worsens when looking up, down, or rotating the head
- Popping or clicking sounds with neck movement

When these symptoms persist for more than a few days, or when pain is accompanied by arm numbness or progressive weakness, a professional evaluation is warranted.
