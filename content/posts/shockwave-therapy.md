## What Is Shockwave Therapy?

Shockwave therapy delivers high-energy acoustic pulses into damaged tissue, breaking down calcifications and scar tissue, increasing blood flow, and activating the body's natural healing cascade. It is non-invasive, requires no anesthesia, and has no significant recovery time.

The technology was originally developed to break up kidney stones and has since been adapted with tremendous success for musculoskeletal conditions. Extensive clinical research supports its effectiveness for chronic tendon and soft tissue injuries that have failed to respond to other treatments.

## Conditions Treated with Shockwave Therapy

### Plantar Fasciitis and Chronic Heel Pain

Plantar fasciitis is one of the most common and frustrating causes of heel pain, often persisting for months despite rest, stretching, and orthotics. Shockwave breaks up calcifications and scar tissue in the plantar fascia and stimulates genuine tissue repair. Studies show success rates of 70–90% for plantar fasciitis that has not responded to conventional care.

### Calcific Tendinitis of the Shoulder

Calcium deposits within the rotator cuff tendons cause severe, recurring shoulder pain. Shockwave disintegrates these deposits non-surgically, providing lasting relief without the risks of injections or surgery.

### Tennis Elbow and Golfer's Elbow

Chronic lateral and medial epicondylitis develop when micro-tears in the forearm tendons fail to heal properly. Shockwave stimulates the degenerative tendon tissue to begin a true healing response, often resolving pain that has persisted for years.

### Patellar Tendinopathy

Athletes and active individuals suffering from chronic knee tendon pain find significant relief with shockwave therapy, which penetrates deep into the patellar tendon to stimulate healing at the cellular level.

### Achilles Tendinopathy

Chronic Achilles tendon pain is notoriously difficult to treat. Shockwave therapy has strong clinical evidence for both mid-portion Achilles tendinopathy and insertional Achilles tendinopathy.

### Chronic Low Back Pain

Shockwave therapy can be used on the muscles and connective tissues of the lower back, addressing trigger points and myofascial restrictions that contribute to chronic lumbar pain.

## What to Expect During Treatment

A typical session lasts 10 to 20 minutes. Dr. Foss applies a gel to the treatment area and delivers shockwave pulses with a handheld probe, targeting the precise areas of damage identified in your assessment. Most treatment protocols involve 3 to 6 sessions spaced one week apart.

During a session, you may feel a tapping sensation and some discomfort as the pulses penetrate into the damaged tissue — this indicates the therapy is reaching the target area. After the session, the treated area may be sore for 24 to 48 hours, similar to a deep tissue massage. This is part of the healing response. Most patients notice meaningful improvement after 2 to 3 sessions.
