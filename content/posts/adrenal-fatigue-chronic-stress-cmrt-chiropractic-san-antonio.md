## The Spinal Control of Adrenals: T9-T11

Your adrenal glands sit on top of your kidneys, right at your mid-back. They are innervated (supplied with nerve signal) by the sympathetic nervous system, which exits the spine at **T9-T11 (thoracic vertebrae 9-11)**.

These adrenal nerves regulate:
- Cortisol production (your stress hormone)
- Adrenaline and noradrenaline (your fight-or-flight hormones)
- Timing of cortisol release (it should be high in morning, low at night)
- Balance between stress hormones and recovery hormones

When T9, T10, or T11 becomes subluxated, the adrenal nerves get irritated. Your adrenals then receive garbled signals:
- Cortisol production becomes chaotic (too high at night, too low during the day)
- Your body stays in a constant "threat" state, flooding your blood with stress hormones
- You cannot come down from the stress response, even when the stressor is gone
- Over months and years, your adrenals exhaust themselves trying to keep up

Result: adrenal fatigue. But the root cause was never the glands themselves — it was the nerve dysfunction causing them to malfunction.

## The HPA Axis: Why Your Whole Stress System is Broken

Here is the bigger picture:

Your **HPA axis** is a three-part feedback loop:
1. **Hypothalamus** (in your brain) senses stress and sends a signal
2. **Pituitary** (also in your brain) receives the signal and tells the adrenals to produce cortisol
3. **Adrenal glands** produce cortisol, which then feeds back to shut down the system

This system is elegant when it works. But when your T9-T11 vertebrae are subluxated, the signal from your adrenals to your brain becomes garbled. Your hypothalamus and pituitary do not get accurate feedback. They think your adrenals are not producing enough cortisol. They keep sending "produce more!" signals.

Your adrenals, obeying orders, keep pumping out cortisol even when you do not need it.

Over time, your adrenals exhaust themselves. You enter "adrenal fatigue" — a state where your glands are so depleted they cannot make enough cortisol even when you need it.

But here is the thing: **fixing the spine often fixes the HPA axis without any need for supplements or hormones.**

Once we restore proper spinal alignment at T9-T11, the adrenals get clear signals again. Your hypothalamus and pituitary can receive accurate feedback. The HPA axis can recalibrate. Your adrenals can recover.

## Chronic Stress and Sympathetic Dominance

Most people with adrenal fatigue are stuck in **sympathetic dominance** — meaning their nervous system is locked in "fight or flight."

In sympathetic dominance:
- You are always vigilant, always tense
- Your digestion shuts down (no stomach acid, slow intestinal movement)
- Your immune system is suppressed (you get frequent infections)
- Your reproductive system shuts down (irregular periods, low libido, infertility)
- Your sleep is broken (cortisol spikes at night instead of melatonin)
- Your memory and focus suffer (cortisol damages the hippocampus)

This is not a personality problem. It is not that you are "too stressed." Your nervous system is physiologically locked in a sympathetic pattern — usually due to spinal misalignment.

CMRT restores parasympathetic tone (the "rest and digest" state), which allows your entire nervous system to downshift. Once you are not in constant fight-or-flight, everything else starts to heal: sleep improves, digestion returns, immunity strengthens, hormones rebalance, mood lifts.
