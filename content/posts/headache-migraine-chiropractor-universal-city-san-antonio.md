## The Structural Causes of Headaches

**Cervical Vertebral Misalignment**
When vertebrae in your neck are out of position, they irritate nerve roots. The irritated nerves fire pain signals up into your head, creating a headache or migraine. Fix the alignment, and the nerve irritation stops.

**Cranial Bone Restrictions**
Your skull is made of 22 bones that move in subtle, coordinated rhythms. When these bones become restricted from stress, poor posture, or past trauma, they restrict nerve and blood flow. This restriction creates referred pain in your head.

**Suboccipital Muscle Tension**
The muscles at the base of your skull — the suboccipitals — are intimately connected to pain pathways. When these muscles are chronically tight, they perpetuate headaches and migraines.

**Forward Head Posture**
Modern life — desks, phones, computers — creates forward head posture. Your head drifts forward, creating constant strain on your neck and upper back. This strain triggers headaches.

**TMJ Dysfunction**
Your jaw joint (TMJ) is connected to your cervical spine and cranium. Jaw misalignment can trigger referred pain into your head.
