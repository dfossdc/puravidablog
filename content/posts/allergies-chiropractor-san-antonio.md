## Understanding Allergies

An allergy is an excessive immune system reaction to a substance (allergen) that's harmless to most people. When you're allergic, your immune system misidentifies an allergen as a threat and launches a defense response, releasing histamine and other inflammatory chemicals that create allergy symptoms.

Common allergens include pollen, mold spores, pet dander, dust mites, certain foods, and environmental chemicals. Symptoms range from mild (itching, slight congestion) to severe (anaphylaxis, severe breathing difficulty).

## Why Some People Have Allergies While Others Don't

The development of allergies involves several factors:

**Genetic Predisposition**: If your parents have allergies, you're more likely to develop them.

**Nervous System Dysregulation**: Stress, spinal misalignments, and neurological dysfunction affect immune system regulation.

**Immune System Imbalance**: Skewing toward excessive inflammatory responses.

**Gut Health**: Intestinal permeability and gut microbiome composition affect immune function.

**Environmental Exposures**: Early childhood exposures influence immune system development.

**Chronic Stress**: Persistent stress dysregulates immune function.

**Spinal Misalignments**: Vertebral subluxations interrupt nerve signals that regulate immune function.

When your nervous system is dysregulated due to stress or spinal misalignments, your immune system often becomes dysregulated as well, leading to excessive allergic responses.
