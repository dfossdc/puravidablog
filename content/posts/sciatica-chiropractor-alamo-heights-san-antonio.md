## Why Sciatica Happens — And Why It Persists

Sciatica is not a condition. It is a symptom of sciatic nerve irritation. The sciatic nerve is the longest nerve in your body, running from your lower back through your buttocks and down both legs. When something irritates or compresses this nerve, you feel sharp, burning, or electric pain — often in one leg, often radiating all the way to your foot.

The most common causes in Alamo Heights residents are:

- **Sacroiliac joint dysfunction** — misalignment at the base of your spine
- **Piriformis syndrome** — tightness in the deep buttock muscle that sits directly on the sciatic nerve
- **Herniated or bulging disc** — disc material pressing on nerve roots
- **Spinal misalignment** — vertebrae out of position compressing the nerve
- **Muscle imbalance** — weak glutes and tight hip flexors creating nerve tension

Here is what matters: most people treat sciatica with painkillers, steroid injections, or rest. These might reduce pain temporarily, but they do not address the underlying structural problem. That is why the pain comes back.

## FAQ

**Should I get an MRI?**
Not always. Most MRIs in sciatica cases show "abnormalities" that are not actually causing pain. If we cannot improve your sciatica within 2-3 weeks, or if you have progressive neurological loss, then imaging is warranted.

**Will you refer me for surgery?**
If conservative care does not resolve your sciatica within a reasonable time, or if you develop progressive weakness or loss of bowel/bladder control, yes — surgery may be necessary. But these cases are rare.

**How long until I am pain-free?**
Most patients experience significant improvement within 2-3 weeks. Complete resolution typically takes 4-8 weeks.

**Can sciatica come back?**
Yes, if you return to the movement patterns or sitting posture that created it. We teach you prevention strategies to keep it resolved.
