## Common Causes of Joint Pain

- **Osteoarthritis (Degenerative Joint Disease)** — cartilage breakdown from age or overuse
- **Bursitis** — inflammation of the fluid-filled sacs that cushion joints
- **Tendinitis** — inflammation of tendons around a joint
- **Sports injuries** — sprains, strains, and impact damage
- **Auto injuries** — whiplash and trauma affecting spinal and extremity joints
- **Inflammatory conditions** — gout, reactive arthritis
- **Poor biomechanics** — misalignment causing uneven joint loading

## Common Symptoms of Joint Pain

Joint pain symptoms vary depending on which joint is affected and the underlying cause. Common presentations include:

- Aching, throbbing, or sharp pain at rest or during movement
- Stiffness that is worst in the morning and improves with activity
- Swelling, warmth, or tenderness around the affected joint
- A grinding, clicking, or popping sensation with movement
- Reduced range of motion — difficulty bending, reaching, or rotating
- Pain that worsens with prolonged activity or after sitting still
- Muscle weakness around the affected joint
- Pain that radiates into nearby areas (hip pain into the groin or thigh, knee pain into the calf)

When joint pain has persisted for more than two to three weeks, is progressively worsening, or is interfering with your daily activities, it is time for a professional evaluation. Early intervention consistently produces better outcomes than waiting until the pain is severe.
