## Why Headaches Happen — And Why Medication Is Not Enough

Most people treat headaches and migraines with painkillers, triptans, or preventive medications. These manage the symptom. But they do not address the underlying cause. That is why the pain comes back as soon as the medication wears off.

Headaches have structural causes:

- **Cervical spine misalignment** — vertebrae out of position irritate nerves
- **Cranial bone restrictions** — bones of the skull compress and restrict nerve and blood flow
- **Suboccipital muscle tension** — tight neck and base-of-skull muscles perpetuate pain
- **TMJ dysfunction** — jaw misalignment affects nearby structures and triggers headaches
- **Postural stress** — forward head posture from desk work creates constant neck strain

When you address the underlying structure, the headaches stop. This is what Dr. Foss does.

## Common Headache Patterns We See

- **Tension headaches** — band-like pressure across the forehead or back of head
- **Migraines** — one-sided, throbbing pain often with light sensitivity and nausea
- **Cervicogenic headaches** — pain originating from the neck and radiating to the head
- **Cluster headaches** — severe, repetitive headaches on one side of the head
- **Rebound headaches** — caused by overuse of pain medication
- **Post-traumatic headaches** — following car accident, fall, or head injury
