## The Immune System Is Regulated by the Nervous System

Here is the key principle that reshapes how we think about children's immunity: **The immune system is not autonomous. It is regulated by the nervous system.**

This field is called **psychoneuroimmunology (PNI)** — the study of how the brain, hormones, and immune system communicate. The science is clear: your nervous system directly controls immune function. When the nervous system is balanced, the immune system functions optimally. When the nervous system is dysregulated, the immune system becomes reactive, overactive, or sluggish.

This is the chiropractic starting point. We do not focus on the immune system itself. We focus on restoring the nervous-system regulation that allows the immune system to work.

## The Autonomic Nervous System: Sympathetic and Parasympathetic Balance

The autonomic nervous system has two branches:

**Sympathetic (Fight-or-Flight).** This system prepares the body for stress and threat. Heart rate increases, blood pressure rises, digestion shuts down, and immune surveillance shifts into high alert. This is useful in true emergencies — but chronic sympathetic activation is inflammatory.

**Parasympathetic (Rest-and-Digest).** This system activates during safety and rest. Heart rate slows, blood pressure normalizes, digestion is active, and the body focuses on repair and regeneration. A healthy parasympathetic tone is anti-inflammatory and supports immune tolerance (the ability to distinguish self from non-self, and not attack the body's own cells).

**Healthy immunity requires parasympathetic dominance.** A child stuck in chronic sympathetic activation develops immune dysregulation — allergies, autoimmune flares, chronic inflammation.

Children today are pushed into sympathetic dominance by stress, screens, lack of outdoor time, processed foods, sleep disruption, and birth trauma that never healed. Restoring parasympathetic tone is foundational to rebuilding immune resilience.

## How SOT Restores Autonomic Balance

**Sacro-Occipital Technique (SOT)** works on the dural-tension system — the relationship between the cranium (head), spine, and sacrum (base of the spine) via the dura mater, the outermost membrane surrounding the brain and spinal cord.

When the pelvis or sacrum is misaligned, it creates dural tension. That tension travels up the spine and affects the brainstem — the "autonomic control center" of the body. Brainstem dysregulation = autonomic dysregulation = immune dysregulation.

SOT uses gentle, low-force corrections to realign the sacrum and pelvis. This releases dural tension, allows the brainstem to recalibrate, and shifts the nervous system toward parasympathetic tone.

For a child with chronic ear infections, allergies, or immune sensitivity, SOT work often brings relief within a few weeks because it addresses the root: nervous-system dysregulation.

Additionally, SOT includes **cranial work** — assessing and correcting restrictions in the cranial sutures. In children, the skull bones are still mobile and respond beautifully to gentle cranial adjustments. Cranial restrictions can impair cerebrospinal-fluid flow and vagal (parasympathetic) function. Releasing them restores immune regulation.

## CMRT and the Visceral-Immune Connection

**CMRT (Chiropractic Manipulative Reflex Technique)** is a specialized approach that identifies reflex points in the body connected to specific organs and systems.

The immune system has direct reflex points: the thymus gland (the master immune gland in young children), the spleen, and lymph tissue. CMRT allows us to stimulate these points, enhance their function, and boost immune resilience.

Additionally, CMRT addresses **gut dysbiosis** — imbalance in the gut microbiome. The gut is home to 70% of the immune system. When the gut microbiome is disrupted (often by antibiotics, processed foods, or stress), immune function suffers. CMRT reflex work can help restore gut signaling and support healthy dysbiosis.

The reflex connections are real, measurable, and powerful — especially in children whose bodies are still plastic and responsive.

## Sources of Immune Dysregulation in Kids

Understanding where immune problems come from helps us address them:

**Birth Trauma.** Forceps, vacuum extraction, and tight nuchal cords create cervical and cranial restrictions that lock the child's nervous system into sympathetic dominance from day one. Early chiropractic care can release these restrictions and reset autonomic tone.

**Chronic Stress.** School pressure, overscheduling, parental anxiety, and social media stress push children into chronic fight-or-flight. The parasympathetic system never gets a chance to activate, so immune tolerance never develops.

**Sleep Disruption.** Screens before bed, inconsistent sleep schedules, and sleep apnea (often undiagnosed in children) rob the body of the parasympathetic recovery window. Poor sleep = poor immune function.

**Antibiotic Overuse.** While antibiotics are sometimes necessary, they devastate the gut microbiome. Each course of antibiotics kills both pathogenic and beneficial bacteria. Without the beneficial bacteria, immune tolerance fails and allergies/sensitivities develop.

**Processed Foods and Gut Inflammation.** Seed oils, refined sugars, and additives drive intestinal permeability ("leaky gut") and dysbiosis. A compromised gut barrier triggers immune reactivity.

**Vaccine Reactions.** Dr. Foss does not advise on vaccine decisions — that is a conversation between you and your pediatrician. But I can say: if a child has a vaccine reaction (fever, excessive irritability, regression in development), chiropractic care that supports autonomic regulation can help the child's body process the reaction more smoothly.

**Environmental Toxins.** Heavy metals, air pollution, and mold exposure dysregulate the immune system.
