Most people who walk into a chiropractic office are looking for pain relief. That's completely understandable. Pain is what gets your attention. Pain is what sends you to Google at 11pm looking for answers.

But pain is rarely the problem. Pain is the signal. The problem — the actual structural dysfunction driving your symptoms — is almost always something deeper.

The Pura Vida Protocol was designed around one guiding principle: **find and fix the root cause, not just the symptom**. Here's what that means in practice.

## What Is the Pura Vida Protocol?

The Pura Vida Protocol is the integrated clinical system Dr. Dan Foss developed over 23 years of practice in San Antonio. It draws on multiple disciplines and advanced techniques to address the full picture of a patient's health — not just the part that hurts.

It's not a single treatment. It's a structured approach to evaluating, diagnosing, and correcting the underlying mechanical, neurological, and biological dysfunction that produces pain and reduces quality of life.

## The Foundation: SOT Chiropractic

At the core of the Pura Vida Protocol is the **Sacro-Occipital Technique (SOT)** — a sophisticated chiropractic system that focuses on the relationship between the base of the spine (the sacrum) and the base of the skull (the occiput). Dr. Foss holds Advanced Certification in SOT through SORSI, one of the highest levels of training available in this technique.

SOT recognizes that the body functions as a system — not a collection of isolated parts. A problem in the pelvis creates compensation in the lumbar spine, which creates compensation in the thoracic spine, which creates compensation in the cervical spine. Fix just the part that hurts and you haven't fixed anything — you've just quieted the alarm while the fire keeps burning.

SOT uses a Category Assessment system to identify the specific pattern of pelvic and spinal dysfunction in each patient, then applies the appropriate correction using specially designed wedge blocks and gravity-assisted adjustments. The result is a precise, gentle correction that produces lasting change because it addresses the pattern, not just the painful segment.

## Cranial Therapy

Many patients — especially those with chronic headaches, migraines, TMJ dysfunction, sinus problems, or neurological symptoms — benefit significantly from cranial chiropractic therapy.

The skull is not a solid, immovable bone. It consists of 22 bones connected by sutures that allow for small but essential movements. The meningeal system — the membranes surrounding the brain and spinal cord — connects the skull to the sacrum through what's called the dural tube. Tension anywhere in this system affects the entire system.

Dr. Foss uses gentle cranial adjusting techniques to restore proper cranial bone motion and reduce meningeal tension. For patients who haven't responded to standard chiropractic care, this is often the missing piece.

## Soft Tissue and Manual Therapy

Structural correction doesn't happen in a vacuum. The muscles and fascia surrounding the spine must be addressed alongside the bones and joints. The Pura Vida Protocol includes specific soft tissue techniques — trigger point therapy, myofascial release, and manual mobilization — to release the muscular tension that locks structural imbalances in place and prevents lasting correction.

## How the Protocol Is Applied

Every patient's journey through the Pura Vida Protocol begins the same way: a complete evaluation. Dr. Foss takes a thorough health history, performs a detailed physical and neurological examination, and reviews any relevant imaging. He uses this information to identify the patient's SOT Category — the specific pattern of dysfunction — and design a personalized care plan.

The protocol progresses through phases:

1. **Initial intensive care** — addressing acute pain and beginning structural correction
2. **Corrective care** — continuing structural work, reducing frequency as stability improves
3. **Maintenance and optimization** — proactive care to maintain results and prevent recurrence

Most patients experience meaningful pain relief within the first few visits. Structural correction — the real goal — takes longer but produces durable results that medication and passive therapy cannot achieve.
