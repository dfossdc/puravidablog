## The Three Pillars of SOT

### Pillar 1 — Spine and Pelvis

Your pelvis is the foundation of your entire structure. If the pelvis is tilted, rotated, or unstable, every structure above it — spine, ribs, shoulders, neck, head — compensates. SOT recognizes three distinct categories of pelvic dysfunction:

- **Category I** — The sacroiliac joint is "fixed" (not moving properly). The dural membranes that wrap your brain and spinal cord are involved. You may feel fatigued, foggy, or just neurologically "off."
- **Category II** — The weight-bearing part of the sacroiliac joint is strained. This is the most common category we see. It often presents as low-back pain that is worse with standing and better with lying down.
- **Category III** — A lumbar disc is involved, usually pressing on the sciatic nerve. This is the classic "sciatica" presentation.

Each category has a specific, reproducible correction using **pelvic blocks** — wedge-shaped tools placed at precise angles under your pelvis. Your own body weight and normal breathing do the adjustment over several minutes. There is no cracking or forceful manipulation required.

### Pillar 2 — Organs (CMRT)

Here is something you were probably never told: your spine is connected to your organs. Every thoracic vertebra — the ones between your shoulder blades — is wired to a specific organ through your autonomic nervous system.

- T5 goes to the stomach (think acid reflux and GERD)
- T9, T10, T11 supply the adrenal glands (think chronic fatigue and burnout)
- T2 through T5 involve the lungs and immune system
- The lower thoracic and lumbar segments connect to the intestines, kidneys, and reproductive organs

When an organ is stressed, the vertebra it is connected to subluxates. And keeps subluxating. Every time you go to your regular chiropractor, he adjusts that same vertebra. A week later it is out again. Why? Because the organ driving it has not been addressed.

**Chiropractic Manipulative Reflex Technique (CMRT)** is the organ-adjusting branch of Advanced SOT. It directly addresses the organ reflex that is holding the vertebra out of place. Dr. Foss is San Antonio's only CMRT-certified chiropractor.

### Pillar 3 — Cranium (SOT Craniopathy)

The 22 bones of your skull continue to move — subtly — throughout your life. This motion drives cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) flow, which bathes and nourishes your brain and spinal cord. When a cranial bone loses its motion — from birth, from a fall, from a concussion, from dental work, from stress-driven muscle tension — CSF flow is disrupted. The nervous system is starved.

The consequences range widely: headaches, migraines, vertigo, tinnitus, TMJ, ear infections in children, post-concussion syndrome, sinus problems, and the classic complaint *"my adjustments do not hold."*

SOT Craniopathy restores this cranial motion with contacts so gentle they are often measured in grams. Many patients describe it as "the doctor just rested their fingers on my head" — and then report dramatic changes afterward.
