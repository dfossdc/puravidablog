## SECTION 1: MISSION STATEMENT

At Pura Vida Chiropractic, our mission is to find the true source of each patient's condition — not simply the location of their pain — by treating the spine, the internal organs, and the cranium as the single interconnected system they have always been. We serve the San Antonio community in English and Spanish, without exception, because every person in this city deserves access to the most thorough and specialized chiropractic care available — care that addresses root causes, restores lasting function, and honors the body's own capacity to heal.

## SECTION 2: CORE VALUES

**1. Root Cause Before Relief**
A symptom is the body's last resort — not its first sign of trouble. We do not measure success by how quickly a patient stops hurting. We measure it by identifying and correcting the actual dysfunction driving the pain, so that relief, when it comes, is real and lasting.

**2. The Whole Body Is the Patient**
The spine does not exist in isolation from the organs, and the organs do not exist in isolation from the cranium. Every examination at Pura Vida assesses all three systems because the source of dysfunction is often not where the symptom appears. Treating the symptom location without understanding the whole body is, at best, incomplete.

**3. Specialization Is a Form of Respect**
Dr. Foss spent years pursuing Advanced SOT certification through SORSI — a post-doctoral curriculum covering the full SOT system, CMRT organ adjusting, and SOT Craniopathy — because patients deserve a practitioner who has gone deeper than the standard education requires. Expertise is not a marketing claim. It is a commitment to the people who trust us with their health.

**4. Language Is Not a Barrier Here**
Pura Vida is a genuinely bilingual practice. Dr. Foss has lived and worked in Costa Rica, Spain, Ireland, the Netherlands, and Guatemala. He does not use a translator. He speaks directly with Spanish-speaking patients in their language because the therapeutic relationship requires real communication, and real communication requires fluency.

**5. Faith-Informed Care**
Dr. Foss practices from a conviction that every person carries inherent worth and that the work of helping someone heal is a form of service. Chiropractic care, at its best, removes interference so the body can do what it was designed to do. That philosophy — that the healing capacity already exists within each patient — shapes the way we approach every adjustment, every visit, every conversation.

## SECTION 3: THE PURA VIDA PHILOSOPHY

**What "Pura Vida" Actually Means**

Pura Vida is a phrase most people associate with Costa Rica — and Dr. Foss first encountered it there, practicing chiropractic after graduating from Western States in 2003. But the phrase is more than a cultural expression. Pura Vida means pure life. Whole life. The life your body is designed to live when the interference holding it back has been removed. It is not an aspirational concept. It is a clinical description of what optimal health actually feels like — a nervous system that communicates clearly, organs that function without compensation, a cranium that moves freely, and a spine that holds its corrections because the forces pulling it out of alignment have been addressed at their source. That is what we are building toward with every patient, every visit.

**Why Standard Chiropractic Often Falls Short**

Most chiropractic education — even excellent chiropractic education — focuses primarily on the spine. And the spine matters enormously. But treating the spine without understanding its relationship to the organs leaves a significant portion of the clinical picture unexamined. When a patient's T5 vertebra keeps subluxating despite repeated adjustments, the question worth asking is: what is pulling it there? The stomach shares its nerve supply with T5. A chronically stressed stomach will reflexively pull that vertebra out of position again and again, and no amount of spinal adjustment will permanently resolve it without addressing the organ driving the dysfunction. The same principle applies to the cranium. When the cranial bones are restricted, the dural tube — which runs from the occiput all the way down to the sacrum — transmits that tension throughout the entire spine. Adjustments placed into that system of tension will not hold. Standard chiropractic, for all its value, rarely addresses either of these layers.

**What Treating a Patient at Pura Vida Actually Means**

When you come to Pura Vida, you are examined as a whole person. The assessment covers SOT category indicators, leg length analysis, arm fossa testing, organ reflex points through Chapman's system, posture analysis, and cranial motion assessment. The treatment that follows is built from that picture — not from a standardized protocol. Some days the priority is the pelvis. Some days the cranium needs attention first. Some days the work is entirely in the organs, and once that reflex resolves, the spine settles on its own. This is what it looks like to treat the body as a system rather than a collection of isolated complaints. It is slower, more thorough, and more demanding of the practitioner — and it is the only approach Dr. Foss has ever been willing to offer.

## SECTION 4: WHAT "ROOT CAUSE" ACTUALLY MEANS HERE

"Root cause" has become a popular phrase in healthcare — but at Pura Vida, it has specific clinical meaning. The viscero-somatic reflex describes the documented neurological pathway by which an organ under stress signals distress through the spinal segment it shares a nerve supply with, causing that vertebra to subluxate. The somato-visceral reflex describes the reverse: a chronically misaligned vertebra stressing the organ at the other end of that nerve pathway. CMRT identifies and treats these reflexes using Chapman's neurolymphatic points and specific organ manipulation, while SOT Craniopathy addresses the cranio-sacral connection that governs tension throughout the entire dural tube from skull to tailbone. When we say root cause, we mean we are looking for the organ or cranial dysfunction pulling your spine out of alignment — not simply adjusting the vertebra that hurts.
