## What Is Shockwave Therapy?

Extracorporeal Shockwave Therapy (ESWT) uses high-energy acoustic pressure waves delivered externally through the skin to target damaged tissue deep within the body. These waves create a controlled microtrauma at the cellular level — a stimulus that "wakes up" the body's innate healing response in areas where chronic injury has caused the tissue to become stagnant and poorly vascularized.

Unlike surgery, there is no cutting, no anesthesia, and no prolonged recovery. Unlike steroid injections, shockwave therapy does not degrade tissue over time — it actively rebuilds it. Many San Antonio patients who have exhausted other options find that shockwave therapy is the key that finally unlocks their recovery.

## How Shockwave Therapy Promotes Healing

The biological mechanisms behind shockwave therapy are well documented. When the acoustic waves penetrate tissue, they:

- **Break down calcific deposits** in tendons and soft tissue
- **Stimulate collagen synthesis** — the structural protein that rebuilds tendons and fascia
- **Promote neovascularization** — the growth of new blood vessels that deliver oxygen and nutrients
- **Disrupt chronic pain signals** — reducing the hypersensitivity of pain receptors in the area
- **Activate stem cells and growth factors** to accelerate tissue repair

The combined effect is not just pain relief — it is genuine tissue regeneration and structural restoration.

## What to Expect During Shockwave Treatment in San Antonio

At our San Antonio clinic, shockwave sessions begin with identifying and marking the precise treatment zones using palpation and patient feedback. A coupling gel is applied to the skin, and the shockwave applicator is pressed firmly against the area and moved in a systematic pattern. Sessions last approximately 10 to 20 minutes depending on the treatment area.

Most patients experience moderate discomfort during treatment — particularly in areas of active pathology — but this typically subsides within minutes of the session ending. A mild, localized soreness for 24 to 48 hours after treatment is normal and indicates the healing response has been activated.
