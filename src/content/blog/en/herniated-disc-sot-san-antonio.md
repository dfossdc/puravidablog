## What Actually Happens With a Herniated Disc

Your spine has 23 intervertebral discs — shock-absorbing pads of fibrocartilage with a gel-like center (nucleus pulposus) surrounded by a tough outer ring (annulus fibrosus). A herniation occurs when the nucleus pushes through a crack in the outer ring.

The most common locations are **L4-L5 and L5-S1** in the lower back (causing leg pain and sciatica) and **C5-C6 and C6-C7** in the neck (causing arm pain and numbness). Disc herniations rarely happen overnight — they are the end result of prolonged biomechanical stress and imbalance.

## How SOT Addresses the Root Cause

### Correcting the Load Imbalance

When your pelvis is misaligned, weight is distributed unevenly across your discs. Certain discs bear far more pressure than they should, accelerating wear and eventually leading to herniation. SOT's signature pelvic wedge technique restores even load distribution, removing excessive pressure from the affected disc and allowing natural healing to begin.

### Low-Force Techniques Safe for Disc Injuries

Unlike high-velocity adjustments that may be inappropriate for acute disc herniations, SOT uses **gentle, low-force methods**. The spinal wedges work with gravity rather than applied force — meaning there's no risk of worsening the herniation during treatment.

### Spinal Decompression Effect

Specific SOT lumbar and cervical techniques create a gentle traction effect that increases the space between vertebrae. This decompression:
- Reduces intradiscal pressure
- Promotes disc rehydration
- Decreases nerve root compression
- Creates conditions for the disc to gradually retract

### Nerve Irritation Relief

Compressed nerves become inflamed, and that inflammation amplifies pain signals. By relieving the mechanical compression, SOT allows your body's natural anti-inflammatory processes to work more effectively, breaking the cycle of pain.

## When Is Surgery Actually Necessary?

Dr. Foss is always straightforward about the limits of chiropractic care. Surgery may be warranted when:

- There is progressive muscle weakness or paralysis
- Cauda equina syndrome is present (loss of bladder/bowel control) — this is a medical emergency
- Intensive conservative care over 3–6 months produces no improvement
- A free disc fragment is causing severe, unrelenting symptoms

Outside of these situations, SOT chiropractic care is the logical — and evidence-backed — first step.
