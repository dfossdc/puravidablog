## What Is SOT Chiropractic?

Sacro-Occipital Technique (SOT) is a comprehensive chiropractic method developed in the 1920s by Dr. Major Bertrand DeJarnette. Unlike standard high-velocity adjustments, SOT focuses on the relationship between the sacrum (the triangular bone at the base of your spine) and the occiput (the base of your skull). These two structures are connected through the dural membrane — the protective sheath surrounding your spinal cord and brain — meaning tension or misalignment in one area directly impacts the other.

SOT uses a system of weighted wedge-shaped blocks placed strategically under the pelvis to allow gravity and your own body weight to gently realign the sacrum and restore normal cranial-sacral motion. The technique is remarkably gentle, making it suitable for patients of all ages and conditions.

## Why SOT Works: The Science Behind the Method

Your spine is not just a stack of bones — it is a dynamic, fluid-filled system that constantly moves with your breathing and heartbeat. The cerebral spinal fluid (CSF) that bathes your brain and spinal cord depends on rhythmic motion between the sacrum and occiput to circulate properly. When that motion is disrupted — due to trauma, poor posture, repetitive stress, or old injuries — the entire nervous system can be affected.

SOT chiropractic restores this essential rhythm. By correcting pelvic and sacral alignment, **Dr. Dan Foss** helps normalize CSF circulation, reduce nerve interference, and allow the body's own healing mechanisms to function at their best.
