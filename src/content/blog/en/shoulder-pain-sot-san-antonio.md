## What Is the Sacro-Occipital Technique?

The Sacro-Occipital Technique (SOT) is a specialized chiropractic system developed in the 1920s by Dr. Major Bertrand DeJarnette. Rather than treating only the site of pain, SOT evaluates the whole body as an interconnected structural and neurological system. The technique centers on the relationship between the **sacrum** (the triangular base of the spine) and the **occiput** (the base of the skull), recognizing that imbalances at these foundational points create compensatory distortions throughout the entire body — including the shoulder girdle.

For San Antonio patients with shoulder pain, this whole-body perspective often reveals contributing factors that standard shoulder treatments never address.

## Why Your Shoulder Pain May Start in Your Spine

Many patients are surprised to learn that their shoulder pain has roots in the cervical and upper thoracic spine. The nerves that control shoulder muscles and sensation travel directly through the neck and upper back before reaching the shoulder joint. When vertebrae in these regions are misaligned — a condition called a subluxation — they can irritate or compress those nerve roots, producing pain, weakness, numbness, and loss of range of motion in the shoulder.

Additionally, SOT analysis frequently reveals pelvic imbalances that create an ascending chain of compensation all the way up into the shoulder girdle. Correcting the foundation corrects the structure built on top of it.

## How SOT Treats Shoulder Pain at Pura Vida Chiropractic

When you visit Dr. Dan Foss for shoulder pain, your care begins with a thorough evaluation: postural assessment, orthopedic and neurological testing, and a full SOT category analysis to determine which patterns of spinal and pelvic dysfunction are present. This evaluation guides every aspect of your individualized treatment plan.

SOT care for shoulder pain typically includes:

- **Pelvic blocking** — padded wedges placed under the pelvis to use the body's own weight and gravity to gently correct pelvic and sacral misalignment
- **Cervical and thoracic adjustments** — precise, low-force corrections to the spinal segments that house the nerves governing shoulder function
- **Extremity adjustments** — direct work on the shoulder, acromioclavicular, and sternoclavicular joints as needed
- **Soft tissue therapy** — to release muscular tension patterns that limit mobility and perpetuate the pain cycle
- **Rehabilitative guidance** — targeted exercises and postural recommendations to maintain your progress between visits

Because SOT uses gentle, measured techniques rather than forceful high-velocity thrusts, it is appropriate for a wide range of patients, including older adults and those with sensitive joints.
