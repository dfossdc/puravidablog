## What Is Class IV Laser Therapy?

Class IV laser therapy — also known as photobiomodulation (PBM) or low-level laser therapy (LLLT) at therapeutic power levels — uses specific wavelengths of light energy to penetrate deeply into tissue, where it interacts with cellular structures to stimulate biological healing processes.

Unlike Class III (cold laser) devices, which emit less than 500 milliwatts of power, Class IV therapeutic lasers operate at power levels ranging from 1 to 25+ watts. This increased power allows energy to penetrate not just into superficial soft tissue, but through muscle and into joints, discs, and bone — making it effective for conditions that have resisted more superficial treatments.

## How Photobiomodulation Works

At the cellular level, the laser light is absorbed by mitochondria — the energy-producing organelles within each cell. This absorption triggers a cascade of beneficial biological effects:

- **Increased ATP production** — cells receive more energy to carry out repair functions
- **Enhanced cellular metabolism** — accelerating the natural healing timeline
- **Reduced inflammation** — by down-regulating pro-inflammatory cytokines
- **Stimulated collagen synthesis** — rebuilding damaged connective tissue
- **Vasodilation and increased circulation** — bringing oxygen and nutrients to injured areas
- **Neurological pain modulation** — reducing pain signal transmission along nerve pathways

The result is faster recovery, reduced pain, improved tissue quality, and restored function.
