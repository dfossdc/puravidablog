## That Shooting Pain Down Your Leg Has a Name — and a Solution

You know the feeling: a sharp, burning, or electric pain that starts somewhere in the lower back or buttock and radiates down the back of your leg, sometimes all the way to your foot. It might be accompanied by numbness, tingling, or a weakness that makes walking or standing difficult. That's sciatica — and if you're dealing with it in San Antonio, you're not alone.

Sciatica is one of the most common and most debilitating forms of nerve pain. It occurs when the **sciatic nerve** — the largest nerve in the body — becomes compressed or irritated somewhere along its path. While many people are told surgery or long-term pain management is their only option, a growing body of evidence supports chiropractic care as an effective, conservative first-line treatment.

## What Causes Sciatica?

The sciatic nerve exits the spinal cord through openings in the lower lumbar vertebrae and sacrum, then passes through the buttock region and down the leg. Irritation or compression can occur at several points along this path:

- **Lumbar disc herniation** — a bulging or ruptured disc pressing against the nerve root
- **Sacroiliac joint dysfunction** — misalignment in the joint where the sacrum meets the pelvis, a very common but under-diagnosed cause of sciatica
- **Piriformis syndrome** — tightening of the piriformis muscle in the buttock, which can compress the sciatic nerve
- **Lumbar spinal stenosis** — narrowing of the spinal canal that pinches nerve roots
- **Pelvic misalignment** — an unlevel pelvis can create uneven pressure throughout the lumbar spine and sacral plexus

SOT is particularly well-suited to treating sciatica because its entire framework centers on the sacrum and pelvis — the exact region where sciatic nerve compression most commonly originates.

## How SOT Addresses Sciatica Specifically

SOT's approach to sciatica goes well beyond adjusting a single joint. It identifies and corrects the full pattern of structural dysfunction that is generating nerve compression:

- **Sacral and pelvic blocking:** SOT's signature pelvic block technique places padded wedges under the pelvis at specific angles to use gravity as a gentle corrective force. This directly reduces stress on the sacroiliac joints and the sacral nerve roots where sciatica often begins.
- **Lumbar decompression:** By restoring pelvic balance and proper lumbar curvature, SOT reduces the mechanical pressure on lumbar discs and nerve roots — allowing compressed or irritated tissues to heal.
- **Piriformis and soft tissue release:** Dr. Foss incorporates targeted soft tissue work to release tension in the piriformis and other deep hip muscles that can trap the sciatic nerve.
- **Sacroiliac joint normalization:** SOT's precision in addressing SI joint dysfunction makes it one of the best chiropractic methods for the significant percentage of sciatica cases that are rooted in SI joint problems rather than disc pathology.
