## Sciatica: The Pain That Follows You Everywhere

If you have ever experienced sciatica, you know exactly how disruptive it can be. The sciatic nerve is the longest and widest nerve in the human body, running from the lower lumbar spine through the buttock, down the back of the thigh, and all the way into the foot. When this nerve becomes compressed or irritated, the resulting pain can be intense — a sharp, burning, or electric sensation that travels through the entire pathway.

Sciatica affects people across San Antonio daily: from workers in physically demanding jobs to desk-bound professionals, from active athletes to retirees. Many people try to manage it with pain medication, rest, or steroid injections — but these approaches rarely resolve the underlying problem. At Pura Vida Chiropractic, Dr. Dan Foss, DC uses the Sacro-Occipital Technique (SOT) to address the structural causes of sciatic nerve irritation at their source.

## What Causes Sciatica?

Sciatica is a symptom, not a diagnosis. It means the sciatic nerve is being impinged somewhere along its path. The most common causes include:

- **Lumbar disc herniation or bulge:** A displaced disc presses against the nerve roots that form the sciatic nerve.
- **Piriformis syndrome:** The piriformis muscle in the buttock tightens around or compresses the sciatic nerve.
- **Sacroiliac joint dysfunction:** An unstable or inflamed SI joint can directly irritate the nerve roots above it.
- **Spinal stenosis:** Narrowing of the spinal canal compresses the nerve.
- **Pelvic misalignment:** An uneven pelvis alters the mechanics of the entire lumbar region, creating conditions for nerve compression.

SOT is particularly well suited to treating sciatica because it directly addresses all of these structural contributors through a systematic, category-based approach.

## How SOT Resolves Sciatic Nerve Compression

**Restoring Pelvic Balance:** The foundation of SOT care is pelvic alignment. When the pelvis is level, stable, and moving properly, it creates the conditions for the lumbar spine to decompress and the sciatic nerve to breathe freely. Dr. Foss uses pelvic blocking — placing specially designed wedge blocks under the pelvis in precise positions — to harness gravity and restore pelvic symmetry without forceful manipulation.

**Sacral Correction:** The sacrum is the keystone of the pelvic ring. When it torques or becomes fixated, it places direct stress on the nearby sciatic nerve roots. SOT sacral techniques release these fixations and restore the sacrum to its proper, stress-free position.

**Lumbar Decompression:** Once the pelvis is balanced, the lumbar vertebrae above it can spread apart and release compressed discs and nerve roots. Many sciatica patients feel significant leg pain relief within their first several SOT sessions as lumbar compression diminishes.

**Piriformis Muscle Release:** SOT includes soft tissue and reflex techniques that address piriformis hypertonicity — an often-overlooked cause of sciatica that originates in the buttock rather than the spine. Dr. Foss can identify and treat this contributor as part of a comprehensive care plan.
