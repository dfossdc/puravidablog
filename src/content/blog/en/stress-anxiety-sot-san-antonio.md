## The Nervous System and the Stress Response

To understand how chiropractic care can help with stress and anxiety, it is important to understand the two branches of the autonomic nervous system. The **sympathetic nervous system** — often called the "fight or flight" system — activates the body in response to perceived threats, raising heart rate, tightening muscles, and suppressing digestion. The **parasympathetic nervous system** — the "rest and digest" system — restores calm, lowers heart rate, and allows the body to repair and recover.

In a healthy, well-regulated body, these two systems balance each other fluidly. Chronic stress, however, keeps the sympathetic system chronically activated, trapping people in a cycle of tension, anxiety, and exhaustion. The spine plays a direct role in this dynamic: when vertebrae are misaligned, they create persistent irritation to the nerve roots that regulate autonomic function, making it structurally difficult for the body to shift into a parasympathetic state.
