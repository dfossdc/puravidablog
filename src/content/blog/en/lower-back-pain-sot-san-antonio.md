## What Is the Sacro-Occipital Technique (SOT)?

The Sacro-Occipital Technique is a comprehensive chiropractic method developed by Dr. Major Bertrand DeJarnette in the 1920s. It is built on a foundational understanding of the relationship between the sacrum (the triangular bone at the base of your spine) and the occiput (the base of your skull). These two structures form the anchors of your spinal column and work together to circulate cerebrospinal fluid throughout your nervous system.

When this relationship is disrupted — through injury, poor posture, repetitive stress, or old trauma — the entire spine can suffer. SOT uses a precise system of analysis, including the use of wedge-shaped blocks placed under the pelvis, to gently coax the body back into its natural alignment. Rather than forcing a correction, SOT allows the body's own weight and gravity to do the work.

## How SOT Addresses Lower Back Pain Specifically

Lower back pain often originates in pelvic imbalance. When the pelvis tilts or rotates unevenly, the lumbar vertebrae above it compensate — leading to compression, disc stress, and muscular tension. SOT categorizes patients into three categories based on their structural patterns and tailors the treatment accordingly.

Here is how SOT helps with common causes of lower back pain:

**Pelvic Imbalance:** SOT blocking techniques restore proper pelvic positioning, relieving the mechanical stress placed on the lower lumbar segments.

**Sacroiliac Joint Dysfunction:** The SI joints are a frequent source of lower back and hip pain. SOT directly addresses sacral mechanics to reduce inflammation and restore joint mobility.

**Lumbar Disc Compression:** By decompressing the sacrum and restoring normal pelvic tilt, SOT reduces the downward pressure on lumbar discs, giving irritated or bulging discs room to heal.

**Muscle Guarding and Spasm:** As structural alignment improves, the surrounding muscles no longer need to overwork to stabilize an uneven frame, leading to natural reduction in spasm and tension.
