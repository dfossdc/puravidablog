## Understanding Fibromyalgia Through a Neurological Lens

Current research increasingly identifies **central sensitization** as a core mechanism of fibromyalgia — a state in which the central nervous system becomes amplified in its pain processing, essentially turning up the volume on pain signals throughout the entire body. While the precise triggers vary by individual, structural factors in the spine and skull can play a significant and often overlooked contributing role.

The **dural system** — the fibrous membrane that envelops the brain and spinal cord — spans from the base of the skull (occiput) all the way to the base of the spine (sacrum). When this system carries abnormal tension, torsion, or restriction, it can generate widespread neurological irritation that feeds directly into the central sensitization process. SOT chiropractic care is designed to address dural system integrity at both ends, making it a uniquely relevant approach for fibromyalgia sufferers.

## Why Conventional Treatments Often Fall Short for Fibromyalgia

Many fibromyalgia patients in San Antonio have tried the standard pharmaceutical options — antidepressants, anticonvulsants, muscle relaxants, sleep aids — and experienced limited, incomplete, or only temporary improvement. The reason these treatments frequently fall short is that they alter the chemical symptoms of central sensitization without correcting the structural inputs that are continuously sustaining it.

SOT care takes a fundamentally different path: rather than modifying brain chemistry, it works to identify and correct the physical and neurological distortions in the spine and cranium that are constantly feeding irritation into the nervous system. When those structural inputs are normalized, the nervous system has a genuine opportunity to down-regulate its hypersensitive state.

## What SOT Treatment Looks Like for Fibromyalgia Patients

Because fibromyalgia patients are often exquisitely sensitive to touch and pressure, the inherent gentleness of SOT is a critical clinical advantage. Dr. Foss does not rely on high-velocity, forceful adjusting techniques. Instead, SOT treatment for fibromyalgia typically includes:

- **Pelvic blocking** — padded wedges placed carefully under the pelvis, using the patient's own body weight and gravity to gently guide sacral and iliac alignment
- **Cranial therapy** — light-touch techniques to normalize motion and release tension in the cranial bones and meningeal membranes
- **Low-force spinal adjustments** — measured, gentle corrections at specific vertebral levels identified through SOT analysis
- **Soft tissue work** — to address muscular guarding patterns that amplify pain and restrict circulation

This patient-centered, low-force approach means that even individuals with severe sensitivity can typically tolerate and benefit from consistent SOT care.
