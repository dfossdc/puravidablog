## Why Structural Misalignment Triggers Head Pain

Your brain and spinal cord are surrounded by cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) and encased in a connective tissue membrane called the **dura mater**. This system has a subtle, rhythmic pumping motion that circulates CSF from the base of the skull to the sacrum and back. When this system is under tension or mechanical stress, it can contribute to pressure-related head pain, neurological irritation, and reduced vascular flow to the brain — all of which are associated with migraine and headache conditions.

The upper cervical spine — especially the atlas (C1) and axis (C2) — is one of the most neurologically sensitive areas of the body. Misalignment here can irritate the trigeminal nerve and other cranial nerves that are closely linked to migraine pathways. It can also restrict blood flow through the vertebral arteries that supply the back of the brain.

SOT is one of the few chiropractic techniques that directly addresses all of these factors.

SOT's approach to head pain is multifaceted and methodical:

- **Neurological normalization:** By removing mechanical interference from the nervous system, SOT supports the brain's ability to regulate pain signals, stress responses, and vascular tone — all of which influence migraine occurrence.
