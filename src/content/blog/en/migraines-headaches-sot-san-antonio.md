## The Connection Between Your Spine and Your Head Pain

SOT is built on a deep understanding of the craniosacral system — the dynamic relationship between the cranium (skull), the spinal column, the sacrum, and the cerebrospinal fluid that circulates throughout. This system plays a critical role in the health of the central nervous system, and when it is disrupted, the effects can manifest as headaches and migraines.

Here are the primary structural contributors to chronic headaches that SOT addresses:

**Cranial Bone Restriction:** The bones of the skull are not fused in a completely rigid structure. They have micro-mobility that allows cerebrospinal fluid to circulate properly. When cranial bones become restricted — through birth trauma, head injuries, dental work, or chronic postural strain — this fluid flow is impeded. Increased intracranial pressure is a well-known trigger for migraines. SOT cranial techniques gently mobilize these bones to restore normal motion and pressure balance.

**Upper Cervical Misalignment:** The upper cervical vertebrae (C1 and C2) are among the most neurologically significant areas of the spine. They sit directly beneath the brainstem and house major vascular and nerve pathways leading to and from the brain. Even minor misalignment at this level can irritate the trigeminal nerve and vertebral arteries — structures directly implicated in migraine generation. SOT addresses upper cervical alignment with precision and care.

**Dural Tension:** The dura mater is a tough membrane that envelops the brain and spinal cord, attaching at the cranium and sacrum. Tension in this membrane — caused by sacral or cranial misalignment — can pull on pain-sensitive structures inside the skull. SOT's simultaneous attention to both the occiput and sacrum is uniquely suited to releasing dural tension along the entire spinal canal.
