## Why Rest Alone Is Not Enough

The traditional advice for sports injuries — rest, ice, compression, elevation — has its place in the acute phase, but it does not resolve the underlying biomechanical imbalances that often allowed the injury to occur in the first place. A slightly rotated pelvis, a restricted spinal segment, an inhibited muscle firing pattern: these structural issues persist after the surface pain fades, leaving the athlete vulnerable to re-injury with every return to activity.

SOT chiropractic care takes a deeper, more complete look at what the body is doing structurally and neurologically, correcting foundational problems so that recovery is thorough rather than temporary.

## How the Sacro-Occipital Technique Benefits Injured Athletes

SOT evaluates the body through three categories that describe how the pelvis, spine, and cranium are functioning as an integrated system. Athletic performance depends on precise movement chains that flow from the feet through the pelvis, spine, and all the way to the skull. When any link in that chain is compromised, compensatory patterns emerge — and those compensations are frequently where injuries occur.

When Dr. Foss assesses an injured athlete using SOT protocols, he identifies which structural imbalances are contributing to the injury and designs a targeted plan. Treatment includes gentle pelvic blocking, precise spinal and extremity adjustments, and cranial work to restore joint mechanics, decompress affected nerves, and re-establish coordinated movement. The result is not just pain relief — it is a genuine restoration of athletic function.
