#### 7. Posting strategy — this is why your impressions are 15-59

Your 6-day-old post got 15 impressions. The 1-month-old "8 in 10 will experience back pain" got 59. That's an algorithm punishment for the wrong format mix.

What's working *less* on LinkedIn in 2026:
- Single-image infographics with a link in the body (LinkedIn algorithmically suppresses external links)
- Generic statistics ("8 in 10 people…")
- Posting once a week or less

What's working *more*:
- 3-7 short paragraphs of personal-voice text, no link in the body (drop the link in the first comment instead — LinkedIn deboosts posts with external links)
- Patient stories told without identifying details ("a mother came in last week…")
- Behind-the-scenes content (a photo from the office, a moment from an adjustment, a CME/training event)
- Polls (highest-reach format on LinkedIn currently)
- Carousels (multi-image posts) — LinkedIn favors these heavily
- Posting 3-5x per week, NOT 1x

Shift your mix to:
- 2 long-form text posts per week (no link, link in first comment)
- 1 carousel per week (3-7 image slides — turn one of your blog posts into a carousel)
- 1 poll per week
- 1 behind-the-scenes photo per week

Combined with the auto-posting pipeline below, this gets you to 5+ posts/week without burning your time.
