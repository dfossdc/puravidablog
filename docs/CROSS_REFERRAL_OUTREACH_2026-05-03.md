## What NOT to do

- **Don't pay for "guaranteed listings."** Some directories charge $50-500 to add you. The good ones (drmartinrosen.com, professional associations) are free or membership-based with real value. Anything that asks for a flat fee just to be listed is usually low-quality.
- **Don't spam the same lawyers.** If a firm doesn't reply after 1 follow-up, move on.
- **Don't pitch directly competing chiropractors for "exchanges."** Reciprocal links between SA chiros signal coordinated link schemes to Google.
- **Don't promise referrals you can't deliver.** If you say you'll send patients, actually send them. The relationships compound.

## Why this pattern beats blog content for backlinks

You could publish 50 blog posts and earn maybe 2-3 organic editorial links. **One Spanish PI lawyer adding you to a 73-practice resource page is worth more for your bilingual SEO than 10 blog posts**, because:
- The link is in the right language (Spanish content)
- The link is in the right geography (SA)
- The link is from a relevantly-themed domain (PI law → injury-related chiro)
- The link is on a page Google already trusts (bilingual + local + active)

Content marketing helps. But for a local service business like yours, **third-party placement on existing trusted resource pages is a far higher-leverage activity** than another 1500-word blog post.
