## Detailed complaint (paste into description box)

```
Pura Vida Chiropractic, PLLC has continuously operated as a licensed chiropractic practice in San Antonio, Texas since 2010, under the mark PURA VIDA CHIROPRACTIC. We have just filed a federal trademark application with the USPTO (Serial No. 99807665, Class 044) on 2026-05-06. We also hold strong common-law trademark rights established by 16 years of continuous public use.

The domain vidasanantonio.com is a typosquat of our domain puravidasanantonio.com. The infringing domain was registered through GoDaddy in May 2024 by a registrant in India.

Pattern of bad-faith registration:

1. The domain is confusingly similar to our established trademark. Removing only the word "pura" from "puravidasanantonio" yields "vidasanantonio." The site even calls itself "Vidas Anantonio" on its homepage — phonetically identical to "Vida San Antonio."

2. The registrant has no business presence in San Antonio, Texas, no chiropractic credentials, no connection to any "Vida" brand, and registered the domain less than two years ago — well after our 16-year common-law mark was established.

3. The website hosts automatically-generated content across categories that have no relation to chiropractic care or San Antonio (Automotive, Education, Lifestyle, Tech, Travel). The content is consistent with AI-generated SEO spam designed to capture and monetize brand traffic.

4. The featured article on the homepage as of 2026-05-06 describes a male chastity device, illustrated with a stethoscope photo apparently selected to confuse visitors who believed they were reaching a medical or chiropractic site.

5. The site is currently outranking the legitimate Pura Vida Chiropractic website in Google search for branded queries — causing direct, measurable confusion among our patients of 16 years and damaging both our practice and our professional reputation.

6. The ranking is provably unearned. A Google site-restricted search for `site:vidasanantonio.com chiropractic` returns "Your search did not match any documents" as of 2026-05-06. A search for `site:vidasanantonio.com "pura vida"` likewise returns zero matches. The infringing site has no chiropractic content, no Pura Vida content, and no San Antonio business identity — its visibility in branded search results is the product of typo-similarity exploitation alone.

This conduct meets the textbook elements of cybersquatting under the Anticybersquatting Consumer Protection Act (15 U.S.C. § 1125(d)) and the elements of bad-faith registration under ICANN's Uniform Domain-Name Dispute-Resolution Policy (UDRP):

(a) The domain is confusingly similar to a mark in which we have rights;
(b) The registrant has no rights or legitimate interests in the domain;
(c) The domain was registered and is being used in bad faith — specifically, to capture traffic intended for our trademark and monetize it through advertising.

We request that GoDaddy:

1. Suspend the domain pending registrant verification;
2. Provide registrant contact information through formal disclosure if a UDRP filing becomes necessary;
3. Forward this complaint to the registrant under GoDaddy's Abuse Policy and the ICANN UDRP framework.

We are prepared to escalate to a formal UDRP filing at WIPO if voluntary compliance is not forthcoming.

Authentic Pura Vida Chiropractic site: https://puravidasanantonio.com
USPTO trademark filing: Serial 99807665 (verifiable at https://tsdr.uspto.gov)
```

## Good-faith certification

GoDaddy's form requires you to certify the information is accurate and that you have a good-faith belief in your claim. All three statements apply.

## After submitting

- GoDaddy responds within 24-72 hours by email with a case number.
- Save the case number and any reply email to this folder as `3-godaddy-correspondence.txt`.
- GoDaddy occasionally suspends domains preemptively for clear typosquatting cases — but more often they forward to the registrant and let the dispute play out via UDRP.

## Escalation path if GoDaddy declines

If GoDaddy does not act, save the rejection email — it strengthens a future UDRP filing because you've documented exhausted attempts at voluntary resolution.

UDRP filing options:
- **WIPO** — https://www.wipo.int/amc/en/domains/ — preferred for international (India) registrants. Cost ~$1,500-2,000.
- **National Arbitration Forum (NAF)** — https://www.adrforum.com — sometimes faster.
