To Whom It May Concern,

I am the applicant on USPTO trademark application **Serial No. 99807665** for the mark **PURA VIDA CHIROPRACTIC** (Class 044, filed 2026-05-06). One day after filing, on 2026-05-07, I received the email forwarded below. The sender purports to be "Peter Macdell, Managing Attorney, Trademark Examination Division" of the USPTO.

I believe this is a fraudulent solicitation impersonating the USPTO and am reporting it for your awareness and action. The indicators that led me to identify it as a scam:

1. Timing is impossible — the email arrived one day after filing, well before any legitimate examination could begin (the USPTO's published timeline is 4-8 months to first examiner review).

2. The letter requests **a valid government-issued identification document, front and back**, of the applicant. This is a request the USPTO does not make and is consistent with identity-theft schemes.

3. The signatory is identified as "Managing Attorney" of the "Trademark Examination Division." Real USPTO Office Actions are issued by a specific Examining Attorney assigned to the application, whose name appears in TSDR.

4. The asserted basis is that the goods/services description is "indefinite and incomplete." My application uses the USPTO ID Manual pre-approved entry **"Chiropractic services"** — by definition that entry is definite. No examiner would call it indefinite.

5. The body uses generic boilerplate threats and TMEP citations rather than identifying specific deficiencies, which is inconsistent with how legitimate Office Actions are written.

6. The disclaimer at the bottom of the email — "USPTO does not communicate with applicants via phone calls" — appears designed to add a veneer of legitimacy while distracting from the email's own irregularities.

I have not responded to the email, will not be sending any identification documents, and am preserving the original message and headers for your investigators.

I would appreciate confirmation that this email did not originate from the USPTO, and any guidance on additional steps you recommend (e.g., FTC report, postal-fraud report, etc.).

Please let me know if you need additional information from me.

## Notes for sending

1. **Add the original email below** by hitting "Forward" in your mail client and pasting this text *above* the original message body. Make sure full headers are included if your mail client supports it (in Gmail: ⋮ menu → "Show original" → copy header → paste at top of forward).

   - U.S. Postal Inspection Service (if the scam ever sends physical mail): https://www.uspis.gov/report

4. **Save the scam email itself** as `trademark-scam-2026-05-07.eml` in this folder for your records — examiner correspondence in real Office Actions sometimes references prior scam attempts on the same application.
