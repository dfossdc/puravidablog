## Why this email matters for our future UDRP filing

This response confirms three facts that will support the UDRP complaint:

1. **GoDaddy is the registrar of record** — confirmed by their citation of "ICANN regulations" governing their conduct. Only registrars are bound by ICANN.
2. **GoDaddy refuses to arbitrate trademark disputes** — they explicitly direct the trademark holder to UDRP or court.
3. **Pre-litigation good-faith effort** — UDRP panels weigh attempts at informal resolution; this email proves we exhausted that channel.

When the UDRP complaint is filed (planned for after federal trademark registration issues from USPTO Serial 99807665), this email becomes Exhibit A under the "Bad Faith — Respondent has been notified and refuses to cease infringement" element.

## Layer-2 plan (UDRP) — to be filed after USPTO registration

- **Timeline:** 60 days from filing to decision.
- **Required elements (we satisfy all 3):**
  1. Domain is identical or confusingly similar to trademark — YES (typosquat of "puravidasanantonio.com" → "vidasanantonio.com" via deletion of "pura").
  2. Registrant has no rights or legitimate interests in the domain — YES (no chiropractic content, no "pura vida" use, registered in India by entity unrelated to San Antonio).
  3. Domain registered and used in bad faith — YES (registered 12 years after our first use; unrelated AI-generated content; clear typosquatting pattern; benefits from our brand traffic).
- **Trigger to file:** USPTO trademark registration certificate issued (expected 2026-Q4 or 2027-Q1).
